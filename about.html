About Taniti

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

FAQs

What type of power outlets are used in Taniti?

Power outlets are 120 volts (the same as in the United States).

Are there restrictions on alcohol sales?

Yes, alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m.

What is the drinking age in Taniti?

The drinking age on Taniti is 18 and the drinking age is not strictly enforced.

Is English commonly spoken?

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. However, very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents.

What are the healthcare facilities like?

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

Is Taniti a safe place to visit?

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. However, as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes, so it's wise to be cautious with your belongings.

How do national holidays affect tourist activities?

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays. Many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on these holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly.

What currency is used in Taniti?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency. Many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
